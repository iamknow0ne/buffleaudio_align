the product

Line up the stack while keeping the performance alive.

Put Align on a Dub, double, harmony, rap layer, or ADR take. Feed the lead as the Guide when your host supports sidechain input, then use the signed offset, Trust Meter, nudge controls, and audition modes to decide whether the layer should move.

studio workflow

Four moves that match how vocal sessions actually run.

01

Pick the role

Choose Double Tight, Choir Natural, Rap Stack, or ADR Loose to start from a musical alignment profile instead of a blank technical control set.

02

Trust the read

The Trust Meter explains whether the Guide and Dub are usable, whether the offset is stable, and whether an early or late correction is worth considering.

03

A/B the change

Switch between Original, Aligned, All Diff, and Tamer so you can hear the dry take, the processed move, the total change, or only the consonant cleanup.

04

Keep it human

Naturalness Guardrail and Articulation Risk flag when a move may be too sterile or when unmatched consonant attacks need a closer listen.

what it does

A transparent timing surface, not a black-box warper.

Align is focused on one practical job: make the timing relationship obvious, help you move a Dub safely, and reveal the artifacts before they land in the mix.

timingSigned Guide/Dub offset, confidence-gated suggested correction, manual nudge, and delay-safe early/late movement in latency-compensated hosts.
articulationConsonant Tamer Lite, Articulation Risk, Naturalness Guardrail, changed-material metering, All Diff audition, and Tamer-only audition.
handoffStack Role presets, phrase-health states, Next Best Move guidance, and Copy Report for sharing the current session read.
